About Me

I am an Electronics and Communication Engineer with a strong specialization in Embedded Systems, IoT, Robotics, and Automation. My experience spans end-to-end development of intelligent electronic systems, including firmware engineering, real-time signal processing, wireless communication integration, PCB design, and system-level testing using STM32, ESP32, and Raspberry Pi platforms. I have worked extensively with protocols such as LoRa, BLE, Zigbee, Wi-Fi, MQTT, and RF communication, enabling reliable and secure IoT connectivity across diverse applications.

Throughout my academic and project journey, I have designed and built highly innovative prototypes, including an AI-based hospital surveillance robot, an EEG-controlled prosthetic arm, a smart wheelchair convertible to a stretcher, autonomous rovers, and multiple smart-city automation systems. These projects demonstrate my ability to integrate hardware, embedded software, sensor fusion, machine learning, and control algorithms into functional real-world solutions.

As the Organizer for Google Developer Group (GDG) and President of the Creators Club at my innovation hub, I have led multiple teams, organized large-scale workshops and hackathons, and mentored students in electronics, coding, robotics, and system design. I have also represented my institute in prestigious competitions, including the IIT Madras Caterpillar Autonomous Vehicle Challenge, showcasing my skills in autonomy, computer vision, obstacle detection, and real-time embedded computing.

My strengths include leadership, problem-solving, rapid learning, technical creativity, and the ability to work calmly under pressure. I am passionate about building next-generation embedded solutions for smart cities, defense systems, healthcare automation, and transportation technology.
